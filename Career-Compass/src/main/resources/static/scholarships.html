Available Scholarships in India

National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship (NMMS)

This is for students in Class 9 to 12 who study in government schools and come from families with lower income. It gives ₹12,000 every year to help continue studies.

Apply

AICTE Pragati Scholarship for Girls

For girl students admitted to AICTE-approved institutions. Provides ₹50,000 per year to support their education.

Apply

AICTE Saksham Scholarship

For differently-abled students with disability of 40% or more. Provides ₹50,000 per year for higher education.

Apply

National Talent Search Examination (NTSE)

For meritorious students up to Ph.D. level. Offers ₹1,250/month for Class 11-12 and ₹2,000/month for UG/PG courses.

Apply

Maulana Azad National Fellowship

For students from minority communities pursuing M.Phil or Ph.D programs. Provides full fellowship support.

Apply

UGC National Fellowship for OBC Students

Financial support for OBC category students pursuing postgraduate or doctoral studies in India.

Apply

JN Tata Endowment for Higher Education

Loan scholarships up to ₹10 lakhs for Indian students pursuing higher studies abroad.

Apply

Fulbright-Nehru Master’s Fellowships

Supports Indian students to pursue master’s degree programs in the U.S. covering tuition, living costs, and travel.

Apply

Commonwealth Scholarships

For Indian students to study postgraduate programs in the UK with full financial assistance.

Apply

Central Sector Scheme of Scholarships (CSSS)

This is for Class 12 students who score well in their board exams and whose family income is not very high. It gives ₹10,000 per year in college and ₹20,000 per year in post-graduation.

Apply

LIC Golden Jubilee Scholarship

This is for students who score at least 60% in Class 10 or 12 and come from low-income families. It gives ₹20,000 to ₹40,000 every year to support further studies.

Apply

INSPIRE Scholarship

This is for Class 12 students who are among the top scorers or have cleared exams like JEE/NEET. It supports students who choose to study science with ₹80,000 every year.

Apply

Swami Vivekananda Merit-cum-Means Scholarship

This is for students from West Bengal studying in Class 11 and above. It helps students from families with less income by giving ₹12,000 to ₹60,000 per year.

Apply

Sitaram Jindal Foundation Scholarship

This is for students from Class 11 onwards whose family income is limited. It gives a monthly allowance between ₹500 and ₹2,500 to help with studies.

Apply

E-Kalyan Scholarship (Jharkhand & Bihar)

For SC, ST, and OBC students from Jharkhand and Bihar. Covers tuition fees and provides maintenance allowance for higher education.

Apply

MahaDBT Scholarship (Maharashtra)

Digital portal offering scholarships for backward classes, minority, and economically weaker students in Maharashtra.

Apply

Kerala State Higher Education Council Scholarships

Merit-based scholarships for UG and PG students in Kerala to promote higher education opportunities.

Apply

Mukhya Mantri Medhavi Vidyarthi Yojana (Madhya Pradesh)

For students scoring 75% or above in Class 12. Provides full tuition fee reimbursement in government and private colleges.

Apply

Odisha State Scholarship Schemes

Multiple schemes for SC, ST, OBC, SEBC, and minority students of Odisha to pursue higher studies.

Apply

Rajasthan Ambedkar International Scholarship Scheme

For SC, ST, OBC, and minority students pursuing Ph.D. abroad. Provides full financial assistance for research studies.

Apply

West Bengal Minority Scholarship (Aikyashree)

For minority students from Class 11 up to Ph.D. level. Covers tuition fees and provides stipends for higher education.

Apply

Delhi Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme

For SC, ST, and OBC category students in Delhi pursuing higher education at college or university level.

Apply

Punjab State Post-Matric Scholarship

For SC and OBC students of Punjab. Provides tuition fee coverage and maintenance allowance for higher studies.

Apply

UP Scholarship (Uttar Pradesh)

One of the largest state scholarships in India. Available for students from school level up to postgraduate courses, based on caste, income, and merit.
